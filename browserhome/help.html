Alternative splicing browserhelp

Tips

1. Use scroll and drag to control the browser,or use left arrow or right arrow to move one step unit.

2. On the top left corner, you can go to a specific coordiante position and also display or undisplay some content.

3. Three different formats are support: ID->include ENST(Ensembl transcript id),uc(UCSC transcript id) and NM(RefSeq id).Exons->user can input the exons and specify the alternative exons. Miso-> miso result format(A3SS,A5SS,Intron retention,cassette exon)

4. We have test it on latest version of Chrome,Firefox,Opera and IE. if it doesn't work properly, maybe you have to update your browser.(Safari has a littler problem about the left top control panel,but not very important)

5. Symbol meaning: 1). Charge n:neutral 2). Charge p:positive 3). Charge g:negative 4). Polarity n:nonpolar 5). Polarity a:acidic polar 6). Polarity b:Basic polar

7. Only transcripts which can be translated into proteins are displayed in 'Gene Structure' modual.

8. Disprot value are caculated using 'VSL2B'

9. The sequence and feature are extracted from UCSC hg19.

10. Only CDS regions are display here.

11. Conservation->"Conservation scores for alignments of 99 vertebrate genomes with Human"

12. We pick the longest isoform when translating miso.

13. This color always means the 'alternative splicing exon'.This color always means the 'constitute exon'.

14. '(x)' in amino acid coordiante means the amino acid position in alternative splicing isoform.
